Do You Know?

Infections that are largely spread through sexual contact are known as sexually transmitted diseases (STDs), also referred to as sexually transmitted infections (STIs). They may be brought on by fungus, viruses, parasites, or bacteria. Here are some general stats on STDs. Vaginal, anal, and oral sex are just a few of the sexual activities that might spread STDs. In addition to sexual contact, some STDs can also be spread through non-sexual activities including sharing needles or coming into contact with bodily fluids that are contaminated. Safe sex practices are the most effective STD prevention strategy. This entails constantly and correctly using condoms, becoming immunized against diseases like HPV and hepatitis B, and undergoing routine checkups and STD screens if you engage in sexual activity. Additionally, it's crucial to avoid sharing needles or other drug paraphernalia and to have open and honest conversations with sexual partners regarding sexual health.
